## Thoughts

Of note, this Thinkpad has the "Aura" edition tag, which means that it has
additional "AI" capabilities. What that really means is that it has an onboard
NPU.

## Why

Though my Chromebook still works _fine_, its age was beginning to show, as it
only includes 8GB of RAM. Having Firefox, Spotify, and a terminal emulator with
`tmux`, multiple instances of `neovim`, and compiling code often makes it run
hot and slow.

I originally bought it with my dad for college for tinkering because I wanted
something lightweight, had decent battery, and had the ability to install Linux.
I was already daily driving my mom's MacBook Air M1 at that point, and I also
had a chunky gaming laptop that I mostly left at home. I wanted a computer that
was light and quiet, yet powerful. Out of the box, ChromeOS does indeed come
with a Linux environment feature, similar to WSL, but it came with its own host
of problems. For one, you could only use Debian which often had outdated
packages. I often found myself depending on `brew` or needing to manually
compile programs like `vim`, which was cumbersome since these should be
automatically updated by the package manager. And two, a Chromebook's internal
components are often underpowered which makes running complex workflows or GUI
programs difficult. This decision was made to balance the price of the computer
and the battery life. It wasn't until I graduated that I fully removed ChromeOS
and was able to install any Linux distribution.
